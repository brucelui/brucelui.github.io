N26 is Europe’s first digital bank; disrupting the traditional way of how we bank. I have the opportunity to be focusing on the most popular feature in the product, “Spaces.”



Spaces are sub-accounts that offers an easy way to manage and track all your finances, and most importantly save money for whatever matters the most.

Unfortunately, my work at N26 is confidential so I cannot show in detail of the work I've done.



THE PROBLEM


what to do with an already successful feature

Spaces was quite a hit already before I joined N26. It has attracted a lot of users to the product and became an iconic differentiator to other competitors, which later became a model for their banking products as well. So with all of this in mind, the next question comes to mind would be naturally:

How do we further engage N26 users to use Spaces?

Increasing adoption rate, expanding use cases, and most importantly retaining the users. These were my goals when I first joined the team as I led the designs for the next era of Spaces.


THE PROCESS

research and data was essential

Along with previous research and data I also conducted my own researches using methods like user interviews, card sorting, usability tests to not only validate concepts but also understanding the overall user journey and generating more insights.

Both qualitative and quantitative data were used to build towards crafting a product strategy and defining on business goals.

seeking out sustainable opportunities

In a fast-paced environment the challenge is always priortization in the product roadmap, so design can play a role where we can challenge the status quo with the insights gathered. I have led a good number of workshops involving from designers to stakeholders to not just define concepts and initiatives we want to explore but also to really dig in on what kind of impact we want to make as a strategy.
